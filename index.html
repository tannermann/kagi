Carnitas

Show less Show more

To make deliciously tender and flavorful carnitas, start by selecting a well-marbled pork shoulder, around 3 to 4 pounds in weight. This cut of meat is ideal for slow cooking, thanks to its rich fat content and deep flavor. Begin by trimming off any excessive fat from the pork—while a bit of fat is essential for taste and moisture, you will want to remove any overly large chunks. Once trimmed, cut the pork into large, rustic chunks, about two inches wide, so they hold up well during the long cook. Lightly coat the pork pieces with a drizzle of avocado oil or extra virgin olive oil, then season them generously with salt and freshly cracked black pepper. To build flavor, sprinkle in a tablespoon each of dried oregano and ground cumin, giving the meat an earthy, aromatic kick. Place the seasoned pork into your slow cooker, nestling it in snugly. Add one small chopped onion and six peeled garlic cloves to infuse the meat with extra depth. Next, pour in the fresh juice from three large oranges and two limes—this citrus bath not only tenderizes the pork but also imparts a bright, tangy note that perfectly balances the richness of the meat. Cover and set your crockpot to cook on low for about eight hours. If you're short on time, you can use a pressure cooker instead—just cook the pork for 45 minutes and allow the steam to release naturally once it is done. After the pork is cooked to perfection—fall-apart tender and swimming in a pool of flavorful juices—it is time for the final, and perhaps most satisfying, step: crisping it up. Heat a heavy skillet over medium-high heat and add a small amount of oil. Take a few chunks of the cooked pork, give them a gentle squish to break them open slightly, and place them in the hot pan. Spoon in a little of the cooking liquid to keep things moist and flavorful as they sear. Let them sit undisturbed so one side gets beautifully caramelized and crispy. Once golden and sizzling, remove the pork from the pan and serve immediately—either on warm tortillas, over rice, or alongside your favorite toppings. These carnitas are tender, juicy on the inside, with irresistible crispy edges that make them an absolute crowd-pleaser.
